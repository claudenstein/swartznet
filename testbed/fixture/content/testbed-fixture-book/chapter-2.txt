Title: The Testbed Chronicles, Volume I
Marker: aethergram
Chapter: 2 — The Customs Officer's Ledger

Port Haversay kept its paperwork in a basement three flights
below the waterline.  Everyone who worked there joked that the
documents were more waterproof than the ships they described, but
the joke was hollow: every winter a handful of entries came up
smelling of brine, and every spring the clerks replaced the lost
pages from memory and a bottle of ink.

Petra arrived with the ingot wrapped in oilcloth.  The clerk at
the counter, a thin woman with a canvas apron and a bored
expression, took one look at the package and rang the brass bell
above her desk.  A door opened in the back of the office and a
short man in a customs coat came out carrying a lantern and a
stub of pencil.

"You want to declare something," he said.  It wasn't a question.

"I want to identify something," said Petra.

"Same form," said the officer, and slid a ledger across the
counter.  The ledger was open to a page headed UNKNOWN ORIGIN.
There were forty-seven entries already, each in a different hand.

"Read them," said the officer.  "If you find a match, we'll talk.
Otherwise we'll file yours as entry forty-eight and you'll come
back in a year and see if anybody else has reported the same."

Petra read.  Each entry described an ingot, a coin, or a small
carved piece of driftwood.  Each carried the aethergram sigil.
None were dated earlier than the long frost.  She set the ingot
on the counter.

"Entry forty-eight," she said quietly.

The officer wrote it down without a word.  When he was done, he
looked up, and Petra saw — for the first time — that he had the
same six-sided mark tattooed on the inside of his left wrist.
