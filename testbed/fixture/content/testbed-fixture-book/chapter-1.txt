Title: The Testbed Chronicles, Volume I
Marker: aethergram
Chapter: 1 — The Silver Ingot

When the silver ingot arrived at the lighthouse on the morning
tide, Petra recognized it immediately as the one her grandfather
had forged the winter before the long frost.  He had stamped each
bar with the aethergram — a six-sided sigil that the customs
officer at Port Haversay always mistook for a watermark.

"It's back," she said aloud to nobody, because the wickerwood
cottage was empty except for the small grey cat that had adopted
her the week she moved up the cliff path.  The cat flicked its
tail and went back to sleep on the wool blanket.

Petra carried the ingot to the study and set it beside the leger
of shipping manifests.  There were exactly forty-seven entries in
the leger, dated between the solstice and the day her grandfather
had rowed out into the fog and had not come back.  The ingot had
not been in any of the forty-seven.  It had come from somewhere
else, from a sea-route that nobody in the wrecking guild talked
about after sundown.

She picked up her pen, turned to a new page, and wrote:
    Entry 48.  One silver ingot.  Aethergram sigil.  Origin unknown.

The cat raised its head, stared at her for a long second, and
then yawned wide enough that Petra could see all four of its
little white teeth.  It was, she would later claim, the most
disrespectful gesture ever made by a cat at the moment a mystery
began.

Outside, the wind shifted.  The lighthouse lamp flickered once,
twice, and resumed its slow sweep over the cove.
